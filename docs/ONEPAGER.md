## The problem we solve

Most commodity desks pay 50-60 k$/yr to Kpler for a feature set they use at 30 % of capacity. Since the 2023-2025 consolidation (Kpler ← MarineTraffic + FleetMon + Spire ; S&P Global ← ORBCOMM AIS), the market has narrower options at the mid-market price point.

**The result for you:**
- ETA broadcasts you can't trust (entered manually by crews)
- Sanctions screening tools sold as separate enterprise add-ons
- No transparent accuracy metric across providers
- Per-port or per-vessel pricing that scales painfully

## What Port Flow gives you

**51 strategic ports** — ARA hub (Rotterdam, Antwerp, Amsterdam), Mediterranean (Algeciras, Fos-Marseille, Genoa, Piraeus), Middle East (Hormuz, Fujairah), Asia (Singapore, Malacca, Shanghai), Americas (Houston, Sabine Pass), bunkering + LNG export hubs.

**Predicted ETA, measured RMSE** — published live on `portflow.uk/precision`. Per-port accuracy benchmarked against broadcast ETA. No marketing math.

**Multi-regime sanctions screening** built in — UKSL (FCDO), OFAC SDN, UN Security Council, EU FSF. Automatic IMO/MMSI reconciliation, visual flag on map + voyage tables.

**Chokepoint transit alerts** — 12 zones tracked (Hormuz, Malacca, Suez, Bab el-Mandeb, Bosphorus, Gibraltar, Dover, Panama, Cape of Good Hope, Magellan, Singapore, Skagerrak-Kattegat). Composable webhook event fires when sanctioned vessel enters.

**Live demurrage risk score** on moored vessels — operational, not academic.

**Alerts** — Slack, Discord, Telegram, email, webhook. Native, no Zapier.

**API + CSV export** from the Starter tier — most competitors gate this at Enterprise.
